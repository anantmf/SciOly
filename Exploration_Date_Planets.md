
Here is a concise list of the first successful spacecraft to visit each major planet (and Pluto), with flyby/encounter dates.

## Inner planets

-   **Mercury – Mariner 10 – 29 March 1974**  
    Mariner 10 (NASA) performed the first flyby of Mercury on 29 March 1974, becoming the first spacecraft to visit the innermost planet.​
    
-   **Venus – Mariner 2 – 14 December 1962**  
    Mariner 2 (NASA) flew past Venus on 14 December 1962, the first successful visit to another planet beyond Earth.​
    
-   **Earth – Various, first artificial satellite Sputnik 1 – 4 October 1957**  
    The first artificial spacecraft to orbit a planet (Earth) was Sputnik 1, launched by the USSR on 4 October 1957.​
    
-   **Mars – Mariner 4 – 15 July 1965**  
    Mariner 4 (NASA) completed the first successful flyby of Mars on 15 July 1965, returning the first close‑up images of the Martian surface.​
    

## Outer gas giants

-   **Jupiter – Pioneer 10 – 3 December 1973**  
    Pioneer 10 (NASA) became the first spacecraft to visit Jupiter, making its closest approach on 3 December 1973.​
    
-   **Saturn – Pioneer 11 – 1 September 1979**  
    Pioneer 11 (NASA) flew by Saturn on 1 September 1979, achieving the first spacecraft encounter with the ringed planet.​
    

## Ice giants

-   **Uranus – Voyager 2 – 24 January 1986**  
    Voyager 2 (NASA) performed the first and so far only flyby of Uranus on 24 January 1986.​
    
-   **Neptune – Voyager 2 – 25 August 1989**  
    Voyager 2 also became the first spacecraft to visit Neptune, with closest approach on 25 August 1989.​
    

## Dwarf planet Pluto

-   **Pluto – New Horizons – 14 July 2015**  
    New Horizons (NASA) made the first flyby of Pluto and its moons on 14 July 2015, providing the first close‑range observations of this dwarf planet.
